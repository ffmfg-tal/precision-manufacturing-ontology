# IP Boundary and Data Classification

## The Problem

The manufacturer is simultaneously:
- A **receiving party** of customer intellectual property (part designs, drawings, specifications, programs)
- A **generating party** of the manufacturer's own proprietary data (process knowledge, tooling, routings, cycle times, cost structures)
- A **transmitting party** to customers (quality records, inspection data, cert packages) and sub-tier vendors (outside process POs, quality flowdowns)

Each data element has a different owner, a different classification, and different rules about where it can live, who can access it, and what can leave the manufacturer's boundary. The digital thread produces a high-density data environment where customer IP, the manufacturer process knowledge, and quality records intermingle in the same job record.

Mishandling this boundary creates:
- **ITAR violations** — exporting controlled technical data without authorization
- **IP contamination** — customer geometry in cloud systems that lack sufficient controls
- **Competitive exposure** — the manufacturer process knowledge transmitted in cert packages or EDI that reveals proprietary methods
- **Compliance failures** — CMMC requirements for controlled unclassified information (CUI) handling

## Data Classification Matrix

### Customer-Owned Data

Data that originates with the customer and cannot be used for any purpose beyond the specific job:

| Data Element | Classification | Storage Rule | Transmission Rule |
|--------------|----------------|-------------|-------------------|
| STEP AP242 model file | Customer IP (often ITAR/CUI) | On-premises or customer-authorized cloud only | Never transmit outside the manufacturer without customer approval |
| 2D Drawing (PDF) | Customer IP | Same as STEP | Transmit to sub-tier vendors only per QA-007 approval |
| Customer NC program | Customer IP | On-premises CNC controller only | Do not copy to external systems |
| Customer part number / revision | Business data | ERP system of record | May appear on ASN, invoice, outside process PO |
| Customer specification list | Customer IP | Job record | May be transmitted to outside process vendors as required |
| Customer quality clauses | Contractual obligation | Job quality plan | Transmitted to sub-tier per QA-007 as flowdown text |
| Customer PO number | Commercial data | ERP | Appears on all outbound documents (ASN, invoice, PO) |

### Manufacturer-Owned Data

Data that is the manufacturer's proprietary process knowledge, generated independently:

| Data Element | Classification | Storage Rule | Transmission Rule |
|--------------|----------------|-------------|-------------------|
| NC toolpath programs (manufacturer-written) | the manufacturer IP | On-premises CNC controller + backup | Do not transmit; not part of cert package |
| Routing (operation sequence, times) | the manufacturer confidential | ERP | Do not transmit; not customer-visible |
| Labor rates, overhead rates, cost | the manufacturer confidential | ERP | Do not transmit; not in cert package |
| Tooling selection and parameters | the manufacturer IP | On-premises | Do not transmit |
| Estimated vs. actual cycle times | the manufacturer operational | ERP / MTConnect ingestion | Do not transmit; internal analytics only |
| Vendor pricing and relationships | the manufacturer confidential | ERP | Do not transmit |
| Material lot cost | the manufacturer confidential | ERP | Do not transmit |

### Quality Records (Jointly Relevant)

Quality data is generated by the manufacturer but pertains to the customer's part. The cert package is transmitted — but only the conformance evidence, not the manufacturer's internal process detail:

| Data Element | What Leaves the manufacturer | What Stays |
|--------------|-----------------|------------|
| CMM results (QIF) | Pass/fail, measured value, deviation per characteristic | Raw probe data, CMM program |
| MTR / CoC from material supplier | Document copy (PDF) | Nothing — it's the supplier's document |
| Process cert from heat treat vendor | Document copy (PDF) | Nothing — vendor's document |
| FAI Forms 1, 2, 3 | All three forms | Internally: NCR history, rework records |
| Certificate of Conformance (manufacturer CoC) | The CoC | Internal: basis for conformance claim |
| NCR records | Customer notified per QA-006 (nonconformance only) | Full NCR/MRB detail stays internal |
| SPC data | Summary Cp/Cpk if QA-017 active | Raw run charts stay internal |

## ITAR Data Handling

ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations, 22 CFR Parts 120-130) controls the export of defense-related technical data. In an aerospace CM shop, ITAR exposure is endemic:

**What triggers ITAR classification at the shop:**
- Any part that appears on the USML (US Munitions List) or is designed/modified for defense articles
- Any part manufactured under a DoD prime contract with ITAR markings on the drawing
- Customer explicitly marks the drawing "DISTRIBUTION STATEMENT: ITAR CONTROLLED"

**ITAR-controlled technical data rules:**
- Cannot be transmitted outside the US (including cloud storage on foreign servers)
- Cannot be accessed by foreign nationals at the shop without an ITAR license or applicable exemption
- Must be stored in ITAR-controlled systems (see CMMC)
- Cannot be included in EDI attachments to foreign parties
- Drawing files cannot be sent to a sub-tier vendor unless they are:
  - A US person
  - Licensed to receive the data
  - Covered by a DSP-5 export license or ITAR exemption

**the manufacturer's ITAR segregation requirements:**
1. ITAR drawings stored only in on-premises systems or ITAR-compliant cloud (FedRAMP High or equivalent)
2. ITAR parts identified in the production system with `itar_controlled: true` flag on item master
3. Outside process POs for ITAR parts: only to vendors with `itar_registered: true` in the production system vendor record
4. MTConnect data for ITAR jobs: machine telemetry (spindle speed, feedrate) is not itself ITAR-controlled; but the context (part number, program name) may be — apply caution
5. QIF results for ITAR parts: dimensional data is controlled if it reveals design intent; transmit only in cert package to authorized recipients
6. Claude MCP: Claude processes production system data including ITAR job data; Claude runs on Anthropic's infrastructure — this is a data residency question to resolve with legal counsel before live deployment on ITAR jobs

## CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification)

For DoD contracts, CMMC Level 2 (or higher) will be required for contractors handling CUI (Controlled Unclassified Information). ITAR-controlled technical data is CUI.

**CMMC requirements relevant to the digital thread:**
- 110 NIST SP 800-171 controls covering: access control, audit logging, configuration management, identification/authentication, incident response, media protection, personnel security, physical protection, risk assessment, security assessment, system and communications protection, system and information integrity
- Cloud systems handling CUI must be FedRAMP Authorized at Moderate baseline or equivalent
- Multi-factor authentication for all accounts with access to CUI
- Audit logs for all access to CUI systems

**the manufacturer's CMMC boundary assessment** (preliminary — not a compliance determination):
- the production system (SaaS ERP): verify the production system's FedRAMP/CMMC status before storing ITAR drawings in the system; currently the production system stores job operational data (P/N, rev, specs), not drawing files
- MTConnect data: resides on shop-floor LAN agents; not directly accessible externally; low CUI risk
- QIF files: temporary transfer format; do not store QIF files containing part geometry in unsecured systems
- Claude / Anthropic API: not currently assessed for CMMC — do not route ITAR-controlled CUI through the Claude API until a compliance determination is made

## What Leaves the manufacturer Per Transaction Type

### X12 850 (Inbound PO) — Customer → Manufacturer
Nothing leaves. Inbound only. PO data creates a production job record.

### X12 855 (PO Acknowledgment) — the manufacturer → Customer
- Confirmed ship date, quantity, price
- No drawings, no process data, no internal records

### X12 856 (ASN) — the manufacturer → Customer
- Shipment details: carrier, tracking, ship date
- Serial / lot numbers for the shipped items
- the production system Job UUID (in REF*ZZ segment)
- No drawings, no MTR, no CMM data — cert package is a separate transmission

### Cert Package — the manufacturer → Customer
- the manufacturer Certificate of Conformance
- Supplier MTR (mill test report) — copy of supplier document
- Supplier CoC from material distributor
- Outside process CoCs (heat treat, plating, NDT)
- FAI Forms 1, 2, 3 (if FAI required)
- QIF inspection summary or dimensional report (not the raw QIF file)
- DFARS declaration (if required)
- SPC Cp/Cpk summary (if QA-017 active)
- **Does NOT include:** NC programs, routings, tooling data, cycle times, cost data, MTConnect telemetry, raw QIF files with full probe data

### Outside Process PO — the manufacturer → Sub-Tier Vendor
- Part number and revision (customer part, transmitted under QA-007 approval)
- Process specification reference (e.g., "AMS 2759/3 to H1025")
- Drawing number and revision (drawing may be attached — ITAR check required)
- Material type
- Quality clause flowdown text
- Required certifications list
- **Does NOT include:** customer PO number (unless required), pricing from customer PO, the manufacturer cost structure

### EDI / Commercial Documents — the manufacturer → Customer Accounts Payable
- X12 810 invoice: line items, amounts, PO reference
- No technical data

## AI Layer Data Residency

The Claude MCP integration operates by querying the production system data through the the production system MCP server. Data routing:

```
Claude (Anthropic cloud) ← [MCP query] → the production system (SaaS cloud) ← [data] → Shop floor
```

**Current assessment:**
- the production system operational data (job status, quantities, dates, costs) is general business data — not CUI for most commercial jobs
- ITAR-controlled CUI (part geometry, classified specs) should not flow through Claude until CMMC assessment is complete
- MTConnect telemetry (spindle RPM, feedrate) is operational data; not itself ITAR-controlled unless inextricably linked to ITAR design data
- QIF measurement results contain dimensional data that may be ITAR-controlled if the geometry is controlled

**Working boundary for Phase 1:**
- Claude queries the production system for job status, material certs, vendor data, inspection pass/fail — all permissible for commercial jobs
- For DoD/ITAR jobs: Claude queries are restricted to operational data (job status, timeline, costs) — not drawing callouts, not dimensional results, not process specifications
- Formal CMMC boundary assessment required before expanding Claude access to DoD job technical data

## Record Retention

Quality records must be retained per customer requirements (typically 10–20 years for aerospace) and regulatory requirements. Retention periods by record type:

| Record Type | Minimum Retention | Governing Requirement |
|-------------|------------------|----------------------|
| FAI records | Life of program + 7 years | Customer contract, AS9100 |
| MTR / material certs | 10 years minimum | AS9100, DFARS |
| DFARS declarations | Duration of contract + 5 years | FAR 4.703 |
| ITAR export records | 5 years | 22 CFR 122.5 |
| Quality inspection records | 10 years typical | Customer contract |
| NCR / MRB records | 10 years typical | AS9100, customer contract |
| SPC data | Duration of program | Customer contract (QA-017) |

the production system records cannot be deleted once a job is closed without compliance review. Archive policy must align with these retention requirements — data is never deleted within the retention window, only archived to read-only status.
